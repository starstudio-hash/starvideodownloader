Understanding video resolution: 1080p to 8K

Video resolution determines how much detail you see on screen. Higher resolution means more pixels, sharper image, and larger file sizes. Here's what each resolution level means:

Resolution Pixels Common name Typical file size (10 min)
1080p 1920 × 1080 Full HD 200-500 MB
1440p 2560 × 1440 2K / QHD 400 MB - 1 GB
2160p 3840 × 2160 4K / UHD 1-4 GB
4320p 7680 × 4320 8K / FUHD 4-12 GB

Why most downloaders can't do 4K

On YouTube (and many other platforms), 4K and 8K video streams are delivered separately from the audio track. This is called DASH (Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP). Simple downloaders that grab a single file URL only get the progressive stream, which typically maxes out at 720p or 1080p.

To get 4K or 8K, a downloader needs to:

  • Detect the separate video and audio DASH streams
  • Download the highest-quality video stream (which may use VP9 or AV1 codec)
  • Download the best audio stream separately
  • Merge them into a single playable file using ffmpeg

HDR video downloads

Some 4K and 8K videos also include HDR (High Dynamic Range) metadata for enhanced color and brightness. HDR comes in several formats:

  • HDR10 — the most common format, supported by most 4K displays
  • HDR10+ — Samsung's dynamic HDR format
  • Dolby Vision — Apple's preferred HDR format, used on iPhone and Apple TV
  • HLG — Hybrid Log-Gamma, used in broadcast TV

Star Video Downloader preserves HDR metadata when downloading. The downloaded file will display in HDR on compatible displays (like Mac with XDR display, or when AirPlayed to an HDR TV).

Codecs: VP9, AV1, and H.264

YouTube's 4K and 8K content typically uses the VP9 or AV1 codec rather than H.264. These newer codecs offer better compression (smaller files at the same quality) but require more processing power to play back.

  • VP9 — widely supported on modern Macs, good balance of quality and compatibility
  • AV1 — best compression, but only hardware-decoded on M3+ Apple Silicon Macs
  • H.264 — universally compatible but rarely available above 1080p on YouTube

If you need H.264 compatibility (for older devices or editing software), use Star Video Downloader's Convert tab to re-encode the downloaded video.

Why are 4K video files so large?

4K video has four times the pixels of 1080p, so file sizes are significantly larger. A 10-minute 4K video might be 2-4 GB depending on bitrate and codec. 8K files can be even larger. Make sure you have enough disk space before downloading.

What's the difference between 4K and HDR?

4K refers to resolution (3840x2160 pixels). HDR (High Dynamic Range) refers to color depth and brightness range. A video can be 4K without HDR, or 4K with HDR. Star Video Downloader preserves HDR metadata when downloading HDR content.
