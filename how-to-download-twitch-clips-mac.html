Why save Twitch content locally?

Twitch doesn't keep content forever. Regular streamers' VODs are deleted after 14 days. Even Partners and Affiliates only get 60 days of VOD storage. Highlights last longer, but they can still be deleted if the streamer removes them or their account is banned.

Clips are more permanent, but they depend on the original VOD existing. If the VOD is deleted, the clip may become unavailable too. If there's a Twitch moment you want to keep — a clutch play, a funny moment, an educational stream — downloading it is the only way to guarantee you'll have it.

Types of Twitch content you can download

Clips

Twitch clips are short highlights (up to 60 seconds) created by viewers or the streamer. They're the easiest to download — just copy the clip URL and paste it. Clips are typically available at the same quality as the original stream.

VODs (Video on Demand)

VODs are full recordings of past streams. They can be anywhere from a few minutes to 48+ hours. Star Video Downloader handles long VODs without issues — it downloads the entire recording as a single file. Remember that VODs expire (14 days for most streamers, 60 days for Partners/Affiliates), so download them before they're gone.

Highlights

Highlights are curated segments that streamers save from their VODs. They last longer than regular VODs but can still be deleted. Download them the same way — copy the URL and paste it.

Past broadcasts vs. live streams

Star Video Downloader works with past broadcasts (VODs) and clips. It cannot record live streams in real-time — you'll need to wait until the stream ends and the VOD becomes available.

Common use cases for downloading Twitch content

  • Content creation — Save clips for compilations, reaction videos, or highlights reels
  • Learning — Keep educational streams (coding, art, music production) for offline reference
  • Archiving — Preserve memorable moments before VODs expire
  • Offline viewing — Watch streams on planes, trains, or anywhere without internet
  • Backup — Streamers can save their own content as a backup in case of account issues
