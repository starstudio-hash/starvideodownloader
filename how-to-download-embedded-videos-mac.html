What is an embedded video?

An embedded video is a video hosted on one platform (like YouTube, Vimeo, or Dailymotion) but displayed within a different website. When a blog or news article shows a YouTube video player inline, that's an embed — the video is streamed from YouTube's servers but appears as part of the page.

Embedded videos are tricky to download because:

  • There's no download button on the embedding website
  • Right-click "Save As" doesn't work with embedded players
  • The video URL isn't visible in the page's address bar
  • The embed may use a custom player that hides the source URL

When the page URL doesn't work

In some cases, the app might not detect the embedded video from just the page URL. Here are some alternatives:

  • Right-click the video: Some browsers show "Copy video address" in the context menu. Use that URL instead.
  • View page source: Press Cmd+U in your browser, then search for "youtube.com/embed", "player.vimeo.com", or ".mp4" to find the video URL.
  • Network inspector: Open Developer Tools (Cmd+Option+I), go to the Network tab, filter by "media", and play the video. You'll see the video URL appear in the requests.
  • Share button: Some embedded players have a share button that reveals the original video URL on the hosting platform.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an embedded video?

An embedded video is a video hosted on one platform (like YouTube or Vimeo) but displayed on a different website using an iframe or embed code. For example, a blog might embed a YouTube tutorial — the video plays on the blog but is actually streamed from YouTube's servers.

Do I need the direct video URL or just the page URL?

Usually the page URL is enough. Star Video Downloader scans the page for embedded video players and extracts the video automatically. If that doesn't work, you can try right-clicking the video player and copying the video URL directly.

Can I download embedded videos from password-protected pages?

Star Video Downloader can download embedded videos from any page you can access. If the page itself requires a login, you may need to provide the direct video URL rather than the page URL, or use cookie-based authentication.

What about videos embedded with custom HTML5 players?

Custom HTML5 players that serve standard video files (MP4, WebM) are generally supported. Star Video Downloader can detect most common video embedding patterns. For very custom or proprietary players, results may vary.
