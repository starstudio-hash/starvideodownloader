What the app is

Star Video Downloader is built for Mac users who want a clean desktop workflow for saving videos from YouTube and 1000+ other sites. Instead of using Terminal commands, copy-paste URLs, quality pickers, queue controls, conversion tools, and repair tools all live inside a native macOS interface.

The app has a free tier for everyday downloads and a one-time Pro upgrade for higher resolutions, playlists, batch input, conversion, repair, SponsorBlock, subtitles, and chapters.

Platform

macOS 14 or later, with native support for Apple Silicon and Intel Macs.

Core stack

SwiftUI for the app interface, yt-dlp for downloading, and ffmpeg for conversion and repair workflows.

Supported sites

YouTube, Reddit, Facebook, Vimeo, TikTok, Instagram, Twitter/X, Twitch, Dailymotion, and many more.

Why it exists

Many video download tools for Mac fall into one of two extremes: powerful but command-line-heavy, or easy-looking but cluttered with ads, bundled software, upload limits, and weak privacy practices. Star Video Downloader exists to bridge that gap with a focused Mac app that feels simple on the surface while still using proven download and media-processing tools underneath.

How it works on Mac

The app uses yt-dlp as its download engine, then uses ffmpeg for post-processing tasks like conversion, audio extraction, and repair. The SwiftUI interface adds queue management, visual quality selection, settings, and productized flows that make those tools practical for people who do not want to work in Terminal.

The result is not a browser trick or a thin wrapper around a website. It is a native desktop workflow designed for Mac users who want local processing and a cleaner experience.
