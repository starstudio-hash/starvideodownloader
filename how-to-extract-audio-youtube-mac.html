Why extract audio from YouTube?

YouTube hosts the world's largest library of audio content — podcasts, music, lectures, interviews, conference talks, and audiobooks. But YouTube doesn't offer a way to download audio files on desktop. You're forced to stream with the video running, draining battery and requiring an internet connection.

Common reasons to extract YouTube audio on Mac:

  • Podcasts — many podcasters publish to YouTube first. Save episodes as MP3 for offline listening
  • Music — save live performances, DJ sets, and unreleased tracks for offline playback
  • Lectures — university lectures and conference talks are easier to review as audio
  • Interviews — save long-form conversations to listen during commutes
  • Language learning — extract audio from language lessons for repeated listening

Supported Audio Formats

Star Video Downloader's converter supports all major audio formats:

MP3

Universal format. Plays everywhere. Good quality at 320kbps with small file sizes.

AAC

Apple's preferred format. Better quality than MP3 at the same bitrate. Ideal for Apple devices.

FLAC

Lossless compression. Full quality, smaller than WAV. Best balance of quality and size.

WAV

Uncompressed audio. Maximum quality. Best for editing and professional use.

OGG

Open-source format. Excellent quality at low bitrates. Popular in gaming and open-source software.

OPUS

Modern, efficient codec. Outstanding quality at very low bitrates. Great for voice recordings.

YouTube to WAV on Mac

If you need the cleanest possible output for editing, archiving, or importing into audio software, WAV is the best YouTube audio format to choose on Mac. It stores uncompressed PCM audio, which makes it ideal for Logic Pro, Final Cut workflows, transcription tools, or any setup where you do not want extra compression.

FLAC gives you the same lossless quality in a smaller file, while MP3 is still the most convenient format for phones, cars, and general playback. Why Not Use Online YouTube-to-MP3 Converters?

Online YouTube-to-MP3 websites may seem convenient, but they come with serious drawbacks:

  • Aggressive ads and pop-ups — deceptive download buttons, auto-playing ads, and malware-laden redirects
  • Malware risk — many sites bundle browser extensions, adware, or worse
  • File size and length limits — can't convert videos longer than 10-20 minutes on most services
  • Quality loss — server-side re-encoding at low bitrates produces noticeably worse audio
  • Privacy concerns — your URLs and browsing habits are logged on someone else's server
  • No lossless formats — online tools never offer FLAC or WAV output
